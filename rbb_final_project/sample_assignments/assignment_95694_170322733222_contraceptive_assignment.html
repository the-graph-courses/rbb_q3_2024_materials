Introduction

Contraceptive use plays a pivotal role in safeguarding reproductive health, and understanding its prevalence within diverse demographic groups is essential for shaping effective public health initiatives. This report delves into the present landscape of contraceptive adoption among women of reproductive age (15-49 years) in Malawi. Its central objective is to delineate the nuanced patterns of contraceptive usage exhibited by women actively employing such measures, considering various social and demographic characteristics.

The exploration of contraceptive use holds profound significance as it directly influences pregnancy outcomes, birth rates, and the capacity of women and their partners to plan and avert unintended pregnancies. Consequently, an in-depth analysis of the prevalent trends within different demographic cohorts allows for a more targeted approach in designing and implementing public health interventions. By shedding light on the specific characteristics that define contraceptive use, this report contributes valuable insights that can inform the development of tailored strategies aimed at promoting reproductive health and facilitating informed decision-making among women in Malawi.

Data Overview

The Demographic and Health Surveys (DHS) are a series of standardized surveys conducted periodically in developing countries that collect data on various aspects of population, health, and nutrition.

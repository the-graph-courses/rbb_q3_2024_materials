Demographic and Health Survey (DHS) data

The Demographic and Health Surveys (DHS) are a series of standardized surveys conducted periodically in developing countries that collect data on various aspects of population, health, and nutrition. Demographic and Health Surveys (DHS) are nationally-representative household surveys that provide data for a wide range of monitoring and impact evaluation indicators in the areas of population, health, and nutrition.This analysis looks at Diarrhea cases distribution in Malawi and its associated factors. it trys to look at the how the hypothesized diarrhea causes are are fairing through data visualization.

Import dataset with haven functions

DHS data comes in the form of a .DTA file (STATA format) and is loaded in R using the haven package. When loading the haven package, other packages that will be used throughout the analysis are also loaded. These packages include tidyverse, janitor, here, Paletteer just to mention a few.

Importing dataset

Data is imported by selecting all the needed columns by using the col_select function since the DHS data set is very huge and it can take alot of time to load all the columns. then we view the data if all the needed variables are in the environment before performing any task.

Relationship between household size and drinking water source

both household size and drinking water source can be the major catalyst of diarrhea if not looked into properly. the chart below looks into that relations to see if the households with large number of people are opting for unsafe water sources thus leading to having cases of diarrhea in the house.for example, public tap/tube well category interms of household size we can see that we have potential outliers i.e some households with over 20 people rely on public sources of water. categoty ids positively skewed which tells us that households with alot of people are using public well asn taps as a source of water. this can indeed make crowded to be exposed to diarrhea.on the other hand we see that households with few people are using bottled water as a source of water for drinking.

### Diarrhea distribution by household size and residential type

based on the box plot below, we see that diarrhea distribution between urban and rural area is not different although when we look at the mean, we that ruralhas a bigger mean than urban. we also observe for rural areas a skewness of the data in the sense that families with large number of people have diarrhea cases than those houses with small size.

Diarrhea cases by resdential type

Looking at the table below, See that more cases are observed in the rural areas than in the urban areas. this can be probably due to lack of proper water sources, poor toilets and other factors associated with rural areas.

Distribution ofdairrhea by stool disposal

looking at diarrhea by stool disposal, we see that highest number of diarrhea cases are happening to people put/in latrines followed by those disposing into drain or ditch.

Distribution of diarrhea by toilet type

looking at the data above, we see that people with pit latrines are experiencing a lot of diarrhea followed by bush defecation. flush toilets people are the least people to be affected by diarrhea.
